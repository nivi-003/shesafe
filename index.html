Safety Tips

Travel Safety

  • Share your live location with a trusted contact before traveling.
  • Prefer well-lit, populated routes especially at night.
  • Keep emergency numbers saved and accessible on your phone.
  • Avoid displaying valuables in public transport.

Online Safety

  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication.
  • Never share personal details with strangers online.
  • Review privacy settings on all social media accounts regularly.
  • Report and block any accounts that harass or threaten you.

Public Places Safety

  • Stay aware of your surroundings and trust your instincts.
  • Sit near the driver or in well-occupied areas on public transport.
  • Have a code word with friends or family to signal distress discreetly.
  • Know the location of the nearest police station or help desk.

Daily Tools

Safety Apps

Recommended apps to keep you safer every day:

  • bSafe — SOS alarm, live GPS tracking, and fake call feature.
  • Shake2Safety — Shake your phone to send an emergency alert.
  • Google Maps Live Share — Share real-time location with contacts.

Fake Call Trick

Use a fake call to exit uncomfortable situations safely:

  • Pre-schedule a fake call using apps like Fake Call or bSafe.
  • Ask a friend to call you at a set time as a planned exit strategy.
  • Use your phone's assistant to set a reminder call to yourself.

Self-Defense Tips

Basic techniques to protect yourself in an emergency:

  • Target vulnerable areas — eyes, nose, throat, and knees — if attacked.
  • Use your voice loudly to attract attention and deter attackers.
  • Carry a personal alarm or whistle for immediate noise deterrence.
